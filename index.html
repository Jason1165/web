The Board

The board consists of 64 spaces and usually have alternating colors of black and white. The board should be set up like this with the queen matching the square.

Setting The Board

The Pieces

On the board there are a total of 6 unique pieces which move differently.
The pawn can only move foward and kills diagonally by one square.
The rook can only move vertically and horizontally.
The bishop can only move diagonally.
The knight moves in a L shape and can jump over pieces.
The queen can move any spaces in each direction.
The king can only move one space in each direction.

Chess Pieces

Checkmate

A checkmate occurs when the king is unable to move anywhere without being attack by an opponents piece or cannot prevent the attack.
The most common kind is a king being trapped by three pieces that attack vertically or horizontally (rook and queen).
